Leader Lines#

In cartography, leader lines are lines used to connect labels on a map to their corresponding feature. They are commonly used when labels cannot be placed directly on the feature without overlapping other labels.

Leader lines are an important tool in map design as they help to improve the readability and clarity of a map by guiding the viewer’s eye to the labeled feature or point of interest.
